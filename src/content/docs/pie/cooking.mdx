**Cooking is where you build.** The PIE Cookbook's *Cooking* section breaks down
into a *primary objective*, *base ingredients*, *other critical ingredients*, the
*oven*, and *recipes*. This is where the catalog plugs directly in: each grouping
maps to a set of vetted, license-checked components.

<Aside type="note" title="From the source">
This adapts the *Cooking → Primary objective / Base ingredients / Other critical
ingredients / Oven / Recipes* section of the
[PIE Cookbook](https://github.com/piepdx/pie-cookbook). The catalog mappings
below are this project's addition.
</Aside>

## The Oven — your environment, infrastructure, and tooling

The oven is what you cook *in*: your dev environment, build tooling, hosting, and
storage. Get this right and everything else is faster; get it wrong and you fight
your tools all day.

<CatalogList pie="The Oven" />

## Recipes — your frameworks and libraries

The main-course ingredients: the frameworks and libraries that make up the body
of what you ship. For Nostr and AT Protocol work this is where the protocol SDKs
live.

<CatalogList pie="Recipes" />

## Catalysts & Pressure — security, identity, and observability

The ingredients that determine whether what you ship is *safe*: cryptography,
key handling, and the monitoring that tells you when something's wrong. For the
communities this catalog serves, these are not optional garnishes.
